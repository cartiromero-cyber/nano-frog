How to Choose a Roof Preservation Company

How to choose a roof preservation company you can trust — the questions to ask, the red flags to avoid, and the standards that matter.

Quick answer

Choose a roof preservation company that inspects before it quotes, publishes honest candidacy standards, documents your roof's condition, uses careful (non-guaranteed) language, and has real reviews. If a company guarantees insurance approval, promises an exact number of added years, or skips the inspection, that's your signal to keep looking.

Green flags

  • Inspection and a condition score before any price
  • Clear standards for which roofs they won't treat
  • Written documentation you keep
  • Honest, careful language
  • Real, verifiable reviews

Red flags

  • Guaranteed years or guaranteed insurance outcomes
  • High-pressure sales
  • No documentation
  • No willingness to say "replace it"

The questions that should disqualify some

Ask: "What roofs do you turn down?" A company that never says no isn't inspecting honestly.

Common questions

What's the most important factor?

Honesty — proven by inspecting first and being willing to recommend replacement.

Are reviews enough?

They help, but pair them with inspection-first behavior and documentation.
