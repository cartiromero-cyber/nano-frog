Does Roof Rejuvenation Really Work?

Does roof rejuvenation / preservation actually work? An honest explanation of when it helps, when it doesn't, and what determines the outcome.

Quick answer

Roof preservation can work — for the right roof. On aging, structurally sound asphalt shingles, treatment is designed to help extend usable life and protect condition. It is not a miracle, it can't fix structural problems, and results depend heavily on candidacy. That's why an honest inspection — not a sales pitch — has to come first.

What determines whether it works

Roof age, granule condition, flexibility, leak history, ventilation, and exposure — the same factors in your Roof Health Score.

When it applies

Best for aging-but-sound roofs in the preservation window. Poor for very new roofs (no benefit yet) or failing roofs (too late).

What to watch out for

Be skeptical of fixed "adds 15 years" promises. Real outcomes vary by roof, and honest providers say so.

Common questions

How long does it last?

It varies by roof and conditions. We avoid guaranteed-year claims and document your roof's actual condition instead.

Will it fix my leak?

No — preservation isn't a repair for active leaks or structural damage. An inspection identifies what your roof actually needs.
