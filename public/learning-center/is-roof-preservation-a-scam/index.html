Is Roof Preservation a Scam? An Honest Answer

An honest look at whether roof preservation is a scam — when it works, when it doesn't, the red flags to watch for, and why inspection comes first.

Quick answer

Roof preservation is not inherently a scam — but the category does attract bad actors, and it's heavily over-marketed. The honest truth: preservation can add usable life to qualifying aging roofs, it does nothing for roofs that are failing or structurally damaged, and it should never be sold without an inspection. If anyone skips the inspection or guarantees results, walk away.

When it works

On aging but structurally sound roofs with life left to protect, preservation is a legitimate option that may extend usable life and document condition.

When it does not

On roofs with structural damage, active failure, missing shingles, or that are past serviceable life, preservation is the wrong answer — replacement is honest.

Red flags to watch for

  • No inspection before a price
  • Guarantees of "X years" or insurance approval
  • High-pressure, today-only deals
  • No written candidacy standards
  • No documentation of your roof's condition
