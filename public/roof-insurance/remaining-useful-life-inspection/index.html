Remaining Useful Life

What a remaining useful life roof inspection is, what it documents, and how it may help aging-roof homeowners. Inspection required; no coverage guarantees.

Quick answer

A remaining useful life (RUL) inspection estimates how many serviceable years your roof likely has left and documents its condition with photos and findings. Some homeowners use that documentation in conversations with insurers. It does not guarantee any insurance decision — insurers evaluate it on their own terms.

What it means

An RUL inspection assesses condition (age, granules, flexibility, leaks, ventilation, exposure) and estimates remaining serviceable years, captured in a written, dated report — our Roof Health Score and Roof Life Report.

When it applies

Useful when your roof is in the age band insurers scrutinize, when you're asked to show condition, or before deciding preserve vs. replace.

What to watch out for

An RUL estimate is a professional opinion, not a promise — and no inspection can guarantee an insurer's decision. Make sure the report is documented and dated.

Documentation may help your conversations with your insurer. We do not guarantee coverage, renewal, or approval — insurers make their own decisions. This page is general information, not insurance advice; speak with your insurer or agent.
