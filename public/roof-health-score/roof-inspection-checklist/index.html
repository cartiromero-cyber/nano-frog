Roof Inspection Checklist

What a thorough roof inspection actually covers — condition, ventilation, flashing, and documentation — and what you should receive afterward.

Quick answer

A real roof inspection covers the whole picture: shingle condition (granules, flexibility, damage), flashing and penetrations, ventilation, signs of leaks or water intrusion, and overall structure — then documents it all in a written, dated report. You should walk away with a Roof Health Score, photos, and an honest recommendation.

What it covers

  • Shingle condition: granules, flexibility, cracking, missing pieces
  • Flashing, valleys, and penetrations
  • Attic/roof ventilation
  • Leak and water-intrusion signs
  • Overall structure and slope/exposure

What you should receive

A Roof Health Score, a documented Roof Life Report with photos, and a clear preserve-or-replace recommendation.

What to watch out for

If an inspection produces no documentation, or skips straight to a quote, that's a red flag.
